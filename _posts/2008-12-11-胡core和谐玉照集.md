胡core和谐玉照集



![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134214438.jpg)  

In 2004, Hu ascended to the Chairmanship of the Central Military Commission,

thus solidifying his portfolio（职位） of power. He had been President of the

People's Republic since 2003 and General Secretary of the Communist Party

since 2002  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134239977.jpg)  

A native of Jiangsu Province, Hu's rise began in northwestern Gansu, where, in

1982, he was promoted to Secretary of the Provincial Communist Youth League  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134311474.jpg)  

Hu (left) is considered part of the "fourth generation" of Chinese leaders. He

was preceded in the job of Communist Party General Secretary by Deng Xiaoping

(second from right) and Jiang Zemin (between them). In this photo, the three

men meet with Party delegates in 1992  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134334672.jpg)  

Hu's ascent was greatly enhanced by his appointment in 1988 to Party Chief of

the restive Tibet Autonomous Region, where he oversaw a political crackdown

the following year. In this photograph he stands next to the Panchen Lama, a

figure appointed by the Chinese to head the government of Tibet in the absence

of the Dalai Lama  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/20086613441343.jpg)  

In 1992, the senior leaders of the Chinese Communist Party tapped（指定、选定） Hu

(center) to join the supremely powerful Politburo, making him the youngest

member of the seven-member committee  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134422729.jpg)  

Hu (grey tie, second from left, front row) joins the other top Chinese leaders

for a 1999 event marking the restitution（归还） of Macau to China  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134450183.jpg)  

Under Hu's leadership, China has expanded its influence in Africa through

investment and trade. In this photo, he arrives for a two-day visit to

Mozambique  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134511347.jpg)  

In 2005, Hu played host to President Bush in Beijing. The summit did little to

alleviate（減輕;緩和） tensions between the two nations over the status of Taiwan,

China's record on human rights and its swelling trade surplus  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134529589.jpg)  

The Chinese leader attends an APEC leaders retreat in 2006  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/20086613469673.jpg)  

The Chinese President arrives at a diplomatic meeting in Hanoi  

  

![](http://www.twis.cn/manage/eWebEditor/Example/NewsSystem/NewsFile/200866134628760.jpg)  

Hu will preside over the first China's first Olympic Games in the summer of

2008

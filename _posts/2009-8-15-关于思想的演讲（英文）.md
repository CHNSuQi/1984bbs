关于思想的演讲（英文）



视频已更新  

  

  

  

I used to spread environmental awareness to fellow students by making

brochures, Webpages and other projects. Now my subject of promotion has

changed. I spread "censorship" awareness in China, mostly to the Chinese

people I know. Rather than print media or the Internet, I spread the awareness

by spoken word, informing them about human rights issues of which most people

are ignorant to at best and indifferent to at worst. I stress the importance

of the public’s attention to these issues.  

The reason this is necessary is due to the restriction of freedom of public

speech. I cannot express my views on censorship on a large scale, because it

will be censored. In attempt address this problem, I am restricted by the

problem itself.  

Even though communicating one-by-one is time-consuming, it is most gratifying

when I see the sparks of thought within each dialogue: people would call past

values into question and start doubt views which were taken for granted. This

is a process I do not often observe when I promoted issues by other mediums. I

believe this is the difference I am establishing for my community: I am not

only informing people of information they cannot access, I believe I can lead

them towards independent thinking, remind them to re-evaluate their knowledge

and to think for themselves. Free-thinking is extremely undermined within the

Chinese education and society. I feel obligated to do something about it. AS

BERGEN EVANS ONCE SAID  

Freedom of speech and freedom of action are meaningless without freedom to

think.  

There is no freedom of thought without doubt.  

  

  

  

  

  

  

  

我刚才想以什么图片或quotation结尾  

搜了一下  

太喜欢马克吐温了。。  

It is by the goodness of God that in our country we have those three

unspeakably precious things: freedom of speech, freedom of conscience, and the

prudence never to practice either of them.  

Mark Twain  

感谢上帝，在我们的国家我们允许那三样无比珍贵的品质存在：言论自由，道德自由，以及永远不需要使用以上两样的小聪明。Mark Twain  

  

最后打算如此结尾  

Like Bergen Evans once insightfully concluded,  

Freedom of speech and freedom of action are meaningless without freedom to

think.  

And there is no freedom of thought without doubt.

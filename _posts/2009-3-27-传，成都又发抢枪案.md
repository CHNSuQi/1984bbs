Mar 27 02:37 AM US/Eastern  

  

  

HONG KONG, March 27 (AP) - (Kyodo)—A sentry was attacked while checking the

identity of an individual approaching a garrison in central China's Sichuan

Province, the second such case after another sentry was shot dead last week in

nearby Chongqing city, a human rights group reported Friday.  

The Information Center for Human Rights and Democracy quoted its sources as

saying the sentry was standing guard at the headquarters of the 149th division

of the Chengdu Military Region in Leshan city on Thursday when the incident

occurred.  

  

After stabbing the soldier in the chest, inflicting only minor injuries, the

attacker fled the scene, the Hong Kong-based center said.  

  

But a Leshan government spokesman said the military had not reported any such

incident, nor was there any such news coverage.  

  

It was not known if the attack was related to the March 19 incident in which

an 18-year-old sentry was shot dead by one or more attackers outside a

garrison in downtown Chongqing.  

  

In that incident, which was reported by state-run media, the sentry was also

robbed of his submachine gun.  

  

The center said military garrisons in Chongging and Sichuan have stepped up

security after the attacks, stacking sandbags and setting up restricted zones,

while shot-to-kill orders have been issued against trespassers who ignore

three warnings.
